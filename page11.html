Spellscaper
AI Powered Platform built in Unreal 5


Project 1: AI Auto-chess(Unity)

When I joined Spellscaper they had the idea of creating a game where generative AI was incorporated into the gameplay. 

Through discussion we came up with the concept of an auto-chess where all abilities were created by the players, using generative AI. Spellscaper at this point consisted of 3 people; CEO, CTO, and me. With me doing implementing most of the gameplay and AI incorporation, and the CTO focusing more on big picture tech.

Over the next 7 months we implemented the auto-chess using Unity as our frontend and our own AI focused "engine" as backend. We reached a point where we could generate 2D worlds, Characters and most importantly: Abilities.

However, generative AI was improving so quickly, that we felt that we didn't utilize its capabilities enough. The concept of player generated abilities wasn't as mind blowing as it was 7 months ago.
This made us think, what if instead of just generating a specific element of a game, the AI generated the entire game?
Project 2: AI Powered Platform (Unreal 5)
We got the idea of creating a platform where a user could for example type "Give me a gun" and "make it shoot apples", and the player would be given an apple gun.
We wanted to create something similar to Roblox, but without an editor and where players could create together online.

After much discussion we decided to switch to Unreal Engine 5 for this project, because it would be much easier to integrate with the AI because its open source.
The reason it was a hard decision to make, was because I was the only one in the company with experience in Unreal. This lead to me being responsible for project structure, making sure best practices were being upheld, etc. 
Over the following months, we created a working prototype of the platform. A player could enter the game and with a couple sentences have a working football game, with two teams, two goals, a ball and an UI showing the score.

- Rapidly prototype and test ideas
