Pawn Avoidance Behaviour

This is a boids based avoidance system with alterations to make the movement more deterministic, minimize overlap between pawns and to stop movement outside of the navmesh

General Overview:

1. Initial Direction
- The pawn gets an initial direction towards its destination.
2. Collision Avoidance
- When a pawn gets within a certain range of another pawn, it checks that pawn's position & direction.
- It then modifies its own direction to avoid the collision.
3. Navmesh Boundary Checking
- To ensure it doesn't leave the navmesh, the pawn queries the navmesh for its current polygon and its polygon next frame if it continues the current direction.
- If it is not allowed to move to that polygon, it counts it as terrain and adjusts its direction accordingly.

Unreal Engine provides its own avoidance behaviour, however this is too floaty and imprecise to be used in a game where the pawn movement matters heavily. It is more suitable for crowd behaviour that is not central to the gameplay.
